Hi! I’m Nikitha, but most people call me Niki. I’m passionate about learning to create software that has a positive impact on our lives. Outside of exploring my interest in coding, you’ll find me playing the piano, taking pictures of food, or discovering a new coffee shop.

Education

I attended Hightstown High School, where I graduated from in June 2021. Here, I was Vice President of my school’s Girls Who Code chapter, and Vice President of the South Asian student association. I was also an active member of DECA, FBLA, National Honor Society, and Spanish National Honor Society, and played on the Girls Tennis team.

Over the summer of 2019, I was chosen from a pool of applicants to join the Program in Algorithmic and Combinatorial Thinking, where I studied a course equivalent to CIS 160 at the University of Pennsylvania. Alongside a group of motivated students from across the world, I gained an understanding of discrete mathematics and theoretical foundations of CS through writing detailed proofs and learning basic graph theory and combinatorial techniques.

I am currently a rising sophomore at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, MI. I am pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Engineering, studying Computer Science through the College of Engineering. On campus, I am involved with the Women in Science and Engineering, Ethical Investments Club, Elementary Engineering Partnerships, and the Indian American Student Association. I plan to graduate in May of 2025!
